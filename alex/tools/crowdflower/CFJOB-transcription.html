Transcription of an audio file.


Instructions

The audio tracks you will be transcribing contain one part of a telephone conversation. The telephone conversation takes place in New York and the caller is looking for a public transport information. In case the track is fuzzy beyond comprehension (e.g. distant echo of the other side), You should fill a symbol that best describes it (table down below).

Please, try to transcribe as well as possible,if there is a faulty transcription found, You will not be able to collect your reward. The test questions are very strict! Please read Your transcription before you send, a lot of typos (from -> fron) etc. It's unfortunate, but it happens a lot and contributors are all annoyed that that's exactly what they've written, but it's not! Listen to it more than once if you're not rock solid!

Write the transcriptions inLOWER CASEwithout punctuation, which means NO COMMAS! (apostrophe allowed) and please, write WORDS FOR NUMBERS.

Example: "goodbye", not "Good bye" and NOT "good-bye"
Example: "you're", not "You're", not "youre" and not "you are" (unless are is explicitly pronounced of course)
Only a-z and ' ( ) are allowed (lowercase, apostrophe and parenthesis).

Write what You hear (or deduce), imagine You're a brilliant Google Automatic Speech Recognizer.

For non-comprehensible tracks please use the following symbols (including the parentheses!):

symbol meaning
(noise) noise on the background
(sil) silence if it is prominent or if there is nothing else to transcribe
(unint) unintelligible speech

To save You a lot of headache, here's words You will hear, don't change spelling, the test questions are uncompromising.
